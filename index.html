My Story

I was raised in an Ecuadorian Indigenous Kichwa family. My passion for technology started thanks to my grandfather, who despite of the lack of formal education, was able to make a living for his family. He gave me my first ever computer, a tiny Toshiba laptop. At the time, I was also a huge fan of books and movies. This made me think of the power that language has to connect ideas. How were we connecting to machines? Later on, my experience with computers shaped my future as a Computer Scientist. However, I never stopped studying languages, philosophy and literature. Now, it all converges in my work as a Natural Language Processing Engineer at ReLuX Tech. Where I focus on building webapps that connect us stretching the limits with AI.

URKU: bringing Ecuadorian Kichwa to NLP

URKU is a LLM proposal fine-tuned in Ecuadorian Kichwa using Low Rank Adaptation techniques. It is based on Meta's LLaMA-2 model (above) and is owned by Universidad San Francisco de Quito as it was part of my thesis research. The project involved the creation of a dataset with multi-disciplinary experts, the fine-tuning of the model and the creation of an expert-based benchmark-like evaluation system. The results were presented at my thesis defense and are currently being prepared for publication.

URKU: Custom GPT version

An alternative GPT-based version (below) was also created in order to compare the results as well as to share it with the community. Otherwise, the model would have never been taken out of the server at the university. It is remarkable to note that the custom GPT creation was various degrees of complexity below the LLaMA-2 fine-tuning process. Still, it was able to generate coherent Kichwa text using only the dataset we had previously curated.
